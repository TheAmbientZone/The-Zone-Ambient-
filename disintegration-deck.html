THE DISINTEGRATION DECK

four tape loops. record something onto a cassette — a built-in melody, your own file, or the microphone (hold the phone near anything: your voice, HAL droning, the rain). the loop plays forever. then raise decay: every pass, the tape itself erodes — duller, gapped, hissier — until the melody you loved is dust. that's the instrument: choosing what to lose, and how slowly.

after basinski · nothing uploaded · the decay is real — record the output, because the loop won't come back

rec ● on a cassette records the selected source onto it (mic asks permission — that's your phone, not the internet). decay 0 = the loop is immortal. decay up = it dies in minutes; the label counts passes and health. ½/1/2 is tape speed, rev flips the reel. stagger two cassettes with the same source at different speeds and let them drift apart — the oldest trick in ambient. ● rec output saves everything to WAV: the only way to keep what the tape is losing.
